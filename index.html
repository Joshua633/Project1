ORIGIN
BY
DAN BROWN

Origin by Dan Brown, published in 2017, is the fifth novel featuring Harvard symbologist Robert Langdon. Set in Spain, the story explores the themes of science, religion, and technology as Langdon seeks to uncover a groundbreaking discovery by futurist Edmond Kirsch that could redefine humanity’s understanding of life’s origins and its future. Kirsch’s revelation is unveiled in a public presentation but is abruptly halted by a shocking assassination. Langdon, joined by museum director Ambra Vidal, races against time to complete Kirsch’s work, facing numerous puzzles inspired by art, history, and scientific advancements. The novel delves into the intersection of belief and scientific progress, challenging readers to question the nature of creation.

book overview

Robert Langdon, Harvard professor of symbology and religious iconology, arrives at the Guggenheim Museum Bilbao to attend the unveiling of a discovery that ‘will change the face of science forever’. The evening’s host is his friend and former student, Edmond Kirsch, a forty-year-old tech magnate whose dazzling inventions and audacious predictions have made him a controversial figure around the world.

about the author

Dan Brown

Dan Brown is an American author known for his suspenseful, puzzle-laden thrillers that often explore themes of science, religion, and conspiracy theories. His most famous work, The Da Vinci Code (2003), became a global bestseller and sparked discussions around religious symbolism and history. Many of his novels feature the recurring character Robert Langdon, a Harvard symbologist, who unravels mysteries within secret societies and ancient codes. Brown's writing is known for blending real historical references with fiction, creating immersive, thought-provoking stories that engage readers worldwide. Other notable works include Angels & Demons, Inferno, and Origin.
